Summary

Meeting Overview

The Housing and Buildings Committee held a hearing examining six bills addressing various housing and infrastructure challenges. The centerpiece was Int 66-2026, sponsored by CM Maloney, which would legalize shared housing - a new typology allowing individual room rentals with shared kitchens and common areas. HPD's Michael Sandler testified that shared housing would create more affordable options than the $4,000/month Manhattan studio apartments currently pricing out single New Yorkers, while addressing the informal and often illegal room-sharing arrangements that already exist citywide. The legislation applies only to new construction and office-to-residential conversions, explicitly prohibiting conversion of existing family apartments to address concerns about reducing family-sized housing stock.

Chair Sanchez pressed HPD on enforcement mechanisms and tenant protections, with HPD confirming that shared housing tenants would have individual leases, right to counsel in eviction proceedings, and rent stabilization protections when built under qualifying tax incentive programs. The committee explored how shared housing might relieve pressure on family apartments currently being subdivided illegally by landlords seeking higher rents from single tenants pooling resources.

The committee also examined Int 71-2026 requiring property owners to repair city trees damaged by scaffolding and sidewalk sheds. DOB's Deputy Commissioner revealed six new sidewalk shed designs developed to replace the outdated 1974 design, with improved aesthetics and reduced visual impact on commercial corridors. Parks Department testified that tree replacement costs several thousand dollars and they receive 100-150 reports annually of construction-related tree damage.

Int 271-2026, addressing tenant services following vacate orders, generated discussion about interagency coordination failures. Chair Sanchez cited a recent fatal fire in her district where communication breakdowns left displaced families in sub-zero temperatures without clear guidance, highlighting systemic problems in emergency response coordination between FDNY, HPD, DOB, and American Red Cross.

Numbers

- $4,000 per month cost of Manhattan studio apartments.
- 40% reduction in development cost per bedroom when converting office buildings to shared housing versus traditional apartments.
- 11% growth in small households between 2013 and 2023.
- 7% growth in small housing units during the same period.
- 670,000 total street trees citywide.
- 25,000 trees estimated in proximity to sidewalk sheds at any given time.
- 100-150 annual reports of construction-related tree damage to Parks Department.
- 46 community-based housing ambassador organizations partnered with HPD.
- 5 years current threshold for proactive enforcement against long-standing sidewalk sheds.
- 6 months typical wait time for housing ambassador appointments in Chair Sanchez's office.
- 30 day minimum lease requirement for shared housing units.
- January 1, 2027 effective date for shared housing legislation.

Action Points

- DOB to circle back with CM Salaam on enforcement procedures for buildings with frequent safety violations.
- DOB and Parks to continue coordinating on most effective approach for tree protection from scaffolding damage.
- DOB to work with CM Zhuang on addressing sidewalk shade regulations for businesses.
- HPD to follow up with committee on good cause eviction protections and unit counting methodology for shared housing buildings.
- HPD to continue internal discussions on fire response coordination and report back to Chair Sanchez on communication protocols.
- DOB to finalize new sidewalk shed designs and share through rulemaking process later this year.
- HPD to work with voucher agencies before January 2027 to establish rental assistance policies for shared housing units.
