NYC Council Committee on Housing and Buildings Meeting Summary

February 9, 2026 | Chair Pierina Ana Sanchez

Meeting Overview

Committee hearing on six housing bills, with primary focus on legalizing shared housing in NYC through comprehensive legislation addressing SROs, office conversions, and tenant protections.

Key Participants

  • Chair Pierina Ana Sanchez and committee members (Hudson, Zhuang, Salaam, Feliz, Maloney, Krishnan, Abreu)
  • Councilmember Virginia Maloney - sponsor of shared housing bill
  • HPD officials: Michael Sandler (Associate Commissioner), Lucy Joffe, Anne Marie Santiago, Emily Osgood
  • DOB: Gus Sarrakis (Deputy Commissioner), Keith Wick
  • Public witnesses: Linlin Liang (Pew Charitable Trust), Jack Connors (Open New York), advocates

Discussion by Agenda Item

Int 0066-2026 (Shared Housing) Maloney argued shared housing addresses gap between full apartments and outdated SROs, noting 40% of New Yorkers already live in shared arrangements. HPD supports bill as response to 11% growth in single households vs. 7% growth in small units. Bill allows new construction and office conversions only - no existing apartment conversions. Tenants would have individual leases, right to counsel, and rent stabilization where applicable. Public testimony raised concerns about bathroom ratios (bill requires 1:3, HUD allows 1:6) and kitchen requirements being too restrictive.

Int 0271-2026 (Vacate Orders) Krishnan's "Back Home Act" bill requires HPD tenant relocation teams after vacate orders. Administration supports concept but opposes mandatory 7A proceedings, preferring current enforcement approach. Chair cited recent Grand Avenue fire where communication breakdown left 10 families displaced in blizzard without proper agency coordination.

Int 0418-2026 (Housing Portal Assistance) HPD opposes in-person portal requirement, citing existing 46 housing ambassador organizations and new library partnerships. Chair noted 6-month wait times for housing ambassadors in her district.

Other Bills - Int 0071: DOB unveiled 6 new sidewalk shed designs, supports tree protection intent - Int 0421: Technical cleanup allowing cellar apartments in new construction
- Int 0427: Re-rental process improvements, current waiver expires April 2026

Notable Facts and Figures

  • 40% of New Yorkers live in shared housing arrangements
  • Manhattan studio apartments average $4,000/month
  • Single households grew 11% vs. 7% growth in small units (2013-2023)
  • 4,000 co-living bedrooms currently operating in NYC (up from zero in 2014)
  • 670,000 street trees citywide, ~25,000 near sidewalk sheds
  • 100-150 annual reports of construction damage to trees
  • 40% reduction in development costs per bedroom for shared housing vs. traditional apartments
  • 46 community-based housing ambassador organizations

Follow-up Commitments

  • HPD to clarify good cause protections application to shared housing units
  • DOB/Parks to determine tree replacement enforcement approach
  • HPD to discuss Grand Avenue fire response breakdown with Chair Sanchez
  • Agencies to provide additional data on vacate order enforcement tools
  • HPD to announce expanded housing ambassador funding plans
