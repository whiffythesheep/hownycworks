Meeting Overview

The Housing and Buildings Committee held an oversight hearing on preserving affordable housing in distress and reviewed Intro 657-2026, dubbed the "Safer Homes Act," which would overhaul the city's dormant Third Party Transfer (TPT) program. Chair Sanchez opened by noting that nearly 10,000 buildings containing 115,000 apartments currently meet severe distress indicators, with serious violations up 40% since 2021, while the TPT program has been suspended since 2019 due to misadministration concerns.

HPD's Rosa Kelly testified that the agency supports reviving TPT with significant reforms, including eliminating the problematic "block pickup" provision that foreclosed entire blocks, replacing the current "statutory distress" criteria with a new "critical eligible" model that targets the worst buildings through an indexing system combining municipal debt and housing violations. The new approach would focus on larger buildings averaging 30 units rather than the previous 10-unit average, with more geographic distribution across the city.

The most contentious exchanges centered on tenant ownership guarantees. Council Members Sanchez and Epstein pressed HPD on why tenants who complete all cooperative training requirements still lack statutory guarantees they'll actually receive ownership, noting that only 47 of 73 tenant petition properties from past rounds have successfully converted to cooperatives, with some waiting over a decade. HPD officials acknowledged the concern but argued for maintaining flexibility rather than statutory mandates, proposing clearer timelines and milestones instead.

Committee members also questioned HPD's request for complete discretion in determining round sizes, the agency's opposition to including vacant lots, and whether buildings with harassment findings should be prioritized. HPD defended its "critical eligible" criteria as objective while acknowledging the need for better outreach, particularly to HDFC cooperatives which comprised 20% of the hypothetical top 500 distressed buildings in their modeling.
