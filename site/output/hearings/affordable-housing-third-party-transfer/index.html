March 9, 2026 - Oversight on Third-Party Transfer Program Reform

Meeting Overview

Committee on Housing and Buildings held oversight hearing on preserving affordable housing in distress and proposed reforms to the Third-Party Transfer (TPT) program via Intro 657-2026 ("Safer Homes Act").

Key Participants

  • Chair Pierina Ana Sanchez (Housing & Buildings Committee)

  • Council Members: Yusef Salaam, Epstein (first name not provided)

  • HPD: Rosa Kelly (Chief of Staff to Commissioner), Lucy Joffe (Deputy Commissioner Policy & Strategy), Annmarie Santiago (Deputy Commissioner Enforcement), Carrie LaBotz (Associate Commissioner Preservation Finance)

  • DOF Representative (unnamed)

Discussion by Agenda Item

TPT Program History and Reform Need

Chair Sanchez outlined program origins in 1976 fiscal crisis when city foreclosed on 5,500 buildings (50,000 units). Current TPT created in 1996, suspended 2019 after problematic Round 10. Now 10,000 distressed buildings with 115,000 apartments, nearly 900,000 serious violations issued in FY24 (40% increase from 2021).

Proposed "Critical Eligible" Model

HPD proposes replacing "statutory distress" criteria with new system targeting worst buildings through indexing municipal debt and Class B/C violations. Would affect ~1% of residential properties (3,000 buildings) vs. broader current criteria. Eliminates problematic "block pickup" provision.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Tax Class 1 properties: debt exceeding 3 years annual tax liability

  • HDFC rentals: debt exceeding 2 years annual tax liability

  • Class 2 rentals: debt exceeding 1 year annual tax liability

Tenant Ownership Concerns

Councilmember Epstein pressed for guarantees that tenants completing cooperative training would receive ownership. HPD cited need for "objective criteria" but couldn't guarantee transfer from third parties. Of 73 tenant petition properties from past rounds: 47 converted, 13 remained rentals, 10 pending conversion, 3 in construction.

HDFC Overrepresentation

HPD data shows ~30% of top 500 distressed buildings are HDFCs (20% co-ops, 10% rentals). Round 10 included 83 HDFC co-ops. New model aims to reduce overrepresentation through refined criteria and exemptions for income-qualifying owner-occupied properties.

Outreach and Notice Improvements

HPD proposes enhanced multilingual outreach, agency liaisons, Owner Resource Center, and multiple notification stages to address past inadequate notice issues, particularly affecting communities of color.

Notable Facts and Figures

  • 350,000 private housing units lost to abandonment in 1960s-70s

  • $2.2 million per building rehabilitation cost in 1970s ($5 million today)

  • 10,000 currently distressed buildings containing 115,000 apartments

  • 900,000 serious violations issued FY24

  • 7,500 homes in 510 properties stabilized through past TPT rounds

  • $625,000-$1.3 million owed per property in top 200-500 distressed buildings

  • $260-312 million total municipal charges for top 200-500 properties

Follow-up Commitments and Outstanding Questions

  • HPD to provide total number of remaining HDFC co-ops citywide

  • Discussion needed on statutory vs. discretionary tenant ownership guarantees

  • HPD to share Neighborhood Restore contract details with committee

  • Refinement needed on exemptions, surplus equity process, and agency liaison vs. ombudsperson roles

  • Outstanding questions on fast-track provisions for repeat AEP buildings and coordination with lien sale reforms
