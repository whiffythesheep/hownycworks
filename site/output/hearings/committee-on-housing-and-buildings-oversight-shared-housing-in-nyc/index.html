Committee on Housing and Buildings, Oversight – Shared Housing in NYC

February 9, 2026 1hr 2m

NYC Council Committee on Housing and Buildings Meeting Summary

February 9, 2026 - Chair Pierina Ana Sanchez led discussion on six housing-related bills and oversight of shared housing.

Key Participants

  • Chair Pierina Ana Sanchez and committee members (Maloney, Salaam, Zhuang, Feliz)
  • HPD officials: Michael Sandler (Associate Commissioner), Lucy Joffe, Anne Marie Santiago, Emily Osgood, Steven Erdman
  • DOB officials: Gus Sarrakis (Deputy Commissioner), Keith Wenk (Assistant Commissioner)
  • Parks Department: Matt Drury (Chief of Legislative Affairs)

Major Agenda Items Discussed

Int 0066-2026 (Shared Housing) - Sponsor CM Maloney - Would legalize shared housing (individual bedrooms with shared kitchens/bathrooms) in new construction and office conversions only - Currently ~40% of New Yorkers live in some form of shared housing, mostly illegally - HPD analysis shows 40%+ reduction in development costs per bedroom vs. traditional apartments - Units would be rent-stabilized under certain tax programs; tenants get individual leases and right to counsel - Does NOT allow conversion of existing apartments to shared housing - Effective date: January 1, 2027

Int 0071-2026 (Sidewalk Sheds and Trees) - Requires permit holders to repair/replace damaged city trees within 6 months - DOB unveiled 6 new sidewalk shed designs in late 2025 to improve pedestrian experience - Current enforcement targets sheds up >5 years

Int 0271-2026 (Vacate Orders) - Would require HPD to initiate Article 7-A proceedings for non-compliant property owners - HPD opposes automatic 7-A requirement, prefers case-by-case approach - Chair cited recent fire at 2264 Grand Avenue highlighting communication breakdowns between agencies

Int 0418-2026 (Housing Portal Assistance) - Would require in-person Housing Connect assistance program - HPD operates 46 community-based housing ambassador organizations - Chair's office reports 6-month wait times for housing ambassador appointments

Notable Facts and Figures

  • Manhattan studio apartments average ~$4,000/month
  • Small households grew 11% (2013-2023) while small units grew only 7%
  • NYC has ~670,000 street trees; ~25,000 near sidewalk sheds at any time
  • Tree replacement costs several thousand dollars
  • 100-150 annual reports of construction-related tree damage

Outstanding Issues

  • HPD temporary re-rental waiver expires April 2026; long-term policy under review
  • Questions remain about good cause protections applicability to shared housing units
  • Need for stronger enforcement tools against worst property owner violators
  • Interagency communication protocols for fire responses require improvement
